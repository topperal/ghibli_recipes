Chihiro`s onigiri

  • 6 mini sheets of roasted seaweed - nori
  • 3/4 cup shredded cooked salmon
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 6 cups cooked sticky rice
  • a triangle shaped cookie cutter

(for 12 rice balls)

  1. Start by slicing your nori sheets in half lengthwise. Mix the mayonnaise into your salmon until well coated. Set the salmon mix and nori aside.
  2. If you`re using freshly cooked rice, wait until the rice is cool enough to handle. If using cold leftover rice, reheat it in a microwave at 30 second intervals until slightly warm—not hot. This way, the rice won`t go cold before you`re finished working with it.
  3. Put the cookie cutter on a flat surface. Place an 8″x8″ sheet of plastic wrap over the cookie cutter and push some of it down inside so that the inside of the cookie cutter is completely lined with plastic wrap. Scoop approximately 2 tbsp of rice into the bottom of the cutter and press it flat with a plastic spoon or small rubber spatula (the rice won`t stick to the tools as much as it sticks to fingers!). When flattening the rice, push it around so that it reaches right up to the corners of the cutter. Place 1 tsp salmon filling into the middle of the rice, spreading it around just a little, so there will be a little salmon in each bite of rice when you`re finished.
  4. Place a couple more tablespoons of rice on top and flatten out as before. Wrap the plastic tightly over the exposed end of the onigiri and pop it out of the cookie cutter. Firmly wrap any loose ends around the outside of the onigiri. Set aside.
  5. Repeat with remaining rice and salmon until you run out of ingredients.
  6. Let the onigiri set for 5-10 minutes. If planning to eat them later, place them on a baking sheet and chill until ready to serve.
  7. When ready to serve, remove the plastic wrap and press a nori strip around the bottom of each onigiri. The rice should still be sticky enough that the nori adheres with no problems.
  8. Now you can eat it! Itadakimasu!
